FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is self-publishing?

It's simple. Self-publishing just means that you decided you want to distribute your work by yourself. What does that mean? You're in charge.

Pros

  • You control everything. Whether you use an editor to whether you create the cover design by yourself. You control how you create the book.
  • All your profits are yours. Except for a small percentage taken by the self-publishing service that you use, you keep everything.

However, there are a few drawbacks as well.

Cons

  • You have to pay for everything. Since you're not working with a publishing house, you have to front all the costs. However, the pro to this is that you retain more profits.
  • You're responsible for marketing. Another great positive of working with a publishing company is the brand and marketing that they help you with to get you into bookstores. When you're self-publishing, you have to do all of this yourself, and it takes a lot of time to get started and build up a brand.

Some people will prefer to send out their manuscript in hopes of getting a publishing contract, but others may just want to go for it and self-publish their novel. It all comes down to what's important to you as an author. Read more about the pros and cons of self-publishing here.

I don't want to use Amazon to publish my eBook. What are the other options?

If you don't want to use Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing, there are many other options available for you to use. Some of the most popular platforms include:

  • Apple Books
  • Barnes & Noble Nook
  • Kobo
  • Smashwords

You can also use a tool like Draft2Digital that lets you upload your manuscript and publish to multiple digital storefronts at one time. Check out the list of Draft2Digital partners to learn more.

Is my manuscript formatted correctly?

Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing supports many different formats.

Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing eBook Format Compatability
Format Tools Compatibility
Microsoft Word (DOC/DOCX) Microsoft Word Fire tablets, Kindle app, Kindle e-ink
Kindle Package Format (KPF) Kindle Create Fire tablets, Kindle app, Kindle e-ink
EPUB Third-party tools Fire tablets, Kindle app, Kindle e-ink
MOBI Third-party tools Fire tablets, Kindle app, Kindle e-ink

For more details about what file formats are supported for Kindle eBook manuscripts, go to the KDP Help Center.
